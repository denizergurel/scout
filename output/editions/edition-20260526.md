RESEARCH

Researchers publishing on arXiv present Realiz3D, a domain-aware learning approach to 3D-consistent image generation that addresses the realism gap between synthetic training renders and real-world photography, using targeted fine-tuning to preserve the photorealistic quality of image generators while maintaining geometry, material, and viewpoint controls.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13852

Published on arXiv, UMo is a real-time co-speech avatar system that unifies gesture and facial animation generation under a single sparse motion model, targeting high-quality expressive digital avatars for games, virtual production, and interactive media applications.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14731

A new arXiv preprint introduces Denoising-GS, a method that improves 3D Gaussian Splatting by addressing noisy Gaussian primitives introduced during optimization from sparse Structure-from-Motion point clouds. Unlike prior approaches that only adjust primitive positions, the method incorporates spatial structure awareness, with potential implications for higher-quality novel view synthesis in XR and immersive content pipelines.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14880

An arXiv preprint presents the Hierarchical Transformer Preconditioner, a neural approach to accelerating real-time physics simulation by anchoring a transformer to a multiscale H-matrix partition to capture long-range physical couplings more efficiently than prior local message-passing methods. Faster and more accurate real-time physics simulation is a foundational requirement for interactive XR environments and spatial computing applications.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13343

Researchers have proposed SyncLight, a method enabling consistent, parametric relighting across multiple uncalibrated camera views of a static scene based on a single edited view, targeting use cases including multi-camera broadcast, stereoscopic cinema, and virtual production. The work addresses a gap in existing generative relighting tools, which struggle to maintain lighting coherence across the multiple viewpoints required for immersive and spatial content.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.16981

A new arXiv preprint introduces R-DMesh, a method for video-guided 3D animation that resolves the pose misalignment problem — where a user-provided static mesh rarely matches the starting pose of a reference video — using a rectified dynamic mesh flow approach. The technique aims to make video-driven 3D animation more practical for content creation workflows in film, games, and spatial computing.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13838

Researchers on arXiv describe a real-time gaze tracking system that uses a microlens array with a co-designed binary chromium mask to perform passive optical encoding, capturing compact task-relevant features for gaze estimation without forming full-resolution images, with potential implications for low-latency eye tracking in XR headsets.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17990

An arXiv paper from cs.HC titled JARVIS proposes a just-in-time augmented reality instruction system powered by vision-language models, designed to deliver in-situ task guidance across reality contexts and reduce the cognitive load of switching between tutorials and hands-on actions. The authors argue that combining AR with LLMs and VLMs can automate the generation of step-by-step guidance that adapts to what the user is doing in the moment.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10108

An arXiv paper in cs.GR presents Density-Sampled Gaussians, a 3D representation that models Gaussian centers as samples from a learnable probability density function over an octree rather than fixed voxel grids or arrays. The authors say the formulation provides a rigorous framework for adaptive density control, bridging adaptive rendering primitives and scalable generative modeling for 3D scenes.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16355

An arXiv paper in cs.GR proposes a decoder-side Gaussian splatting approach for immersive video that aims to ease pixel-rate bottlenecks by avoiding the transmission of high-resolution depth maps or explicit 3D volumetric data. The authors argue the method addresses limitations of decoder-side depth estimation with complex geometries, inter-view flickering, and non-Lambertian reflections while retaining the view-synthesis quality of 3D Gaussian Splatting.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17002

An arXiv paper in cs.GR proposes accelerating 3D Gaussian Splatting by mapping its compute-bound rasterization stage onto modern GPU tensor cores, targeting the per-pixel Gaussian contribution evaluations that dominate end-to-end rendering time. The authors frame the work as addressing latency-sensitive uses of 3DGS for real-time neural rendering and 3D scene reconstruction.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17855

Researchers on arXiv present 3D Skew Gaussian Splatting, an extension of 3D Gaussian Splatting that replaces symmetric Gaussian kernels with skewed distributions to better capture shape and color discontinuities, reducing blurriness and primitive redundancy during real-time photorealistic view synthesis.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18334

A paper presented at ICRA and posted to arXiv describes a method for turning standard iPhones into spatial data capture rigs, leveraging the device's sensors to gather 3D scene data for robotics and computer vision applications. The work suggests consumer hardware can serve as a low-cost platform for spatial dataset collection traditionally requiring specialized equipment.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05945

Researchers on arXiv present MMGS, a compression framework for 3D Gaussian Splatting that uses optimal transport aggregation based on multi-view ranking to achieve roughly tenfold compression while preserving rendering fidelity, addressing a key bottleneck for deploying 3DGS scenes on bandwidth- and memory-constrained XR devices.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19304

A new arXiv paper proposes a decentralized, browser-native WebGPU architecture for direct volume rendering of MRI-based digital twins, aiming to replace server-side rendering and expensive local workstations to make interactive, patient-specific anatomical models viable for surgical planning in resource-constrained settings.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19737

A Hacker News submission highlights RuView, an open-source project exploring WiFi-based spatial sensing, part of a broader research thread investigating how ambient wireless signals can be used to infer presence, motion, and room geometry without dedicated cameras or depth sensors.

https://github.com/ruvnet/RuView
